What We Do

Everything we do at OLH is designed to build sustainable funding for the digital publication and preservation of open access humanities scholarship.

Day-to-day, this means developing open source software, overseeing article publication, researching new possibilities for scholarly communication, and engaging our community of librarians, editors, and researchers. Our work is built on four key actions.

Publish

Hosting 28 peer-reviewed academic journals (and counting!), including our flagship Open Library of Humanities journal.

We oversee editorial processes and provide support to our hosted editors, to ensure the highest standards of research integrity. As our library supporters grow, we expand our portfolio by ‘flipping’ subscription journals to diamond open access.

Develop

Building Janeway, a powerful, flexible, emphatically open source publishing platform.

Having our own software makes OLH journals sustainable, and gives us independence from commercial publishing platforms. We listen to our journal editors and continually improve the platform with scholars’ needs in mind.

Research

Mobilising our affiliation with Birkbeck, University of London, to ensure that OLH is truly scholar-led.

Co-founder Martin Paul Eve continues to research scholarly communications and has published books like Open Access and the Humanities (2014) and Password (2016). Co-founder Caroline Edwards researches and teaches on models of collectivity and utopianism at Birkbeck.

This specialism extends to our wider team. Our Editorial team use their doctorates in humanities subjects, and extensive experience of peer review, to steer robust and quality open access research to publication. We also continue our scholar-led tradition through UX research into what our users want, continually improving our software to put scholars in charge.

Engage

Rallying our global community of librarians, editors, and academics around our shared vision of not-for-profit humanities publishing.

We deliver talks, participate in working groups and committees, write, lead, and collaborate, to champion open access as an equitable and sustainable alternative to commercial publishing. We also present awards to recognise exemplary contributions to the OA community (and we’ve won a few of our own).
